Working Principle of Cooling Towers

Cooling towers use evaporative cooling, where heat is transferred from water to air through evaporation and direct heat exchange. This process cools down the water used in industrial processes or HVAC systems.

Step-by-Step Working Principle
  1. Hot Water Distribution: Warm water is pumped in...
  2. Air and Water Interaction: Airflow is introduced...
  3. Heat Transfer Mechanisms: Evaporative cooling and direct heat exchange.
  4. Cooling of Water: The water's temperature drops...
  5. Release of Warm, Moist Air: Heat and moisture exit...
  6. Recirculation: The cooled water returns to the process...

Key Components: Fill Material, Fans, Drift Eliminators, Nozzles, Cooling Basin. The core principle is using the latent heat of vaporization: as water evaporates, it absorbs a large amount of heat from the remaining water, lowering its temperature.

Applications: Power plants, Chemical plants, HVAC systems, Petroleum refineries, etc.
